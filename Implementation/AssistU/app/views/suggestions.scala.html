    In research (and in your every day life) you acquire knowledge and experience. You start with observations that arise questions that need to be answered and shared. The steps that lead from an observation to a valid, ready to be made public, answer form a methodology known as “the scientific method”.

    If you have results that deserve to be published, you should stop and think who is the audience for your next-coming article. You will find it most probably by looking at the journals from your reading list. The cited articles that help building the context of your own research, will give you a target list of journals where you can publish. Choose carefully and maximise the impact of your results.

    Introduction

    Show more...

    The introduction should give a concise, up-to-date description of the background to provide a general reader sufficient context to understand the research being presented and its significance. It should provide a clear statement of the research question and any hypothesis being explored. Please address explicitly the ageing aspect(s) discussed in your paper.

    Use relevant resources to give a brief and balanced review of relevant literature on your subject.

    Materials & Methods

    Show more...

    In the Materials and methods body, the techniques, materials and equipment should be described in sufficient detail for another trained researcher to be able to reproduce the experimental work reported. Methods that are identical to published work and procedures should be still briefly described and referred to. For non-experimental papers equivalent demands exist for the methods applied and data sources (e.g. archives, questionnaires).

    Abstract, keywords & Title

    Show more...

    The abstract should not exceed a maximum of 120 words. The content should state the main purposes and research questions of the study, the methods used, the main results and key conclusions obtained. Keywords will be used for indexing purposes. A maximum of five keywords should be given.

    The title page should include a concise and informative title (maximum 2 lines), the name(s) of the author(s), including symbols linking the author name with relevant affiliation. The name of the corresponding author is linked with a footnote for contact details.
